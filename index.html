The Problem

Most building gyms never get used.

01

Quality equipment, zero guidance

Residents show up motivated but have no organized way to maximize the facility. Without direction, newcomers can feel lost and stop coming. A room full of machines is not a fitness amenity.

02

No accountability, no results

Without structure and follow-through, residents stop before they start. The gym sees less engagement and the amenity quietly loses its value to the building.

03

A clear separator from buildings with gyms

Prospective residents might ask what makes your building different. A personalized fitness app tied to actual programming modeled after the exact equipment in your gym is a real answer.

04

One-size-fits-all doesn't fit anyone

A beginner and a former athlete need completely different programs. AmenityFit adjusts automatically with no added equipment or manual input required from building staff.

Can I skip the Romanian deadlifts today? My hamstrings are still sore.
Soreness from yesterday is expected. That is muscle adaptation, not injury. The RDLs today are lighter and actually help flush the soreness. Go through them. Reduce range of motion on the first set if it feels sharp and you will feel better by set two.
That actually makes sense. How much weight should I use?
Start at 60 to 65% of what felt challenging last week. You are in a volume phase. The goal is reps at controlled tempo, not max load. 3 sets of 10.
Wearables

Training that responds to your actual recovery.

AmenityFit connects to residents' wearables and uses real recovery data to automatically adjust workout intensity in the background. No manual input. No guesswork.

Recovery-adjusted intensity

When recovery is low, the program pulls back automatically. When it is strong, full intensity loads. The workout adapts to the person on that specific day.
